A magnetron requires a high-voltage DC supply, typically a few kilovolts, to sustain the
crossed-field electron emission that generates microwave power. For the AtomCraft RF heating
system that supply operates around 4 kV — enough that a single unsafe interaction can be
lethal. This project covered the circuit simulation, physical packaging, and safety framework
for that supply.

<Callout title="Design scope">
This page covers **simulation and design work**: LTspice circuit exploration, Fusion 360
enclosure design, and safety procedure planning. It does not claim a complete validated
build unless further evidence is added.
</Callout>

## Circuit simulation in LTspice

The supply uses a full-wave rectifier topology: mains input, a step-up transformer, a
diode bridge, and a large filter capacitor bank to smooth the DC rail. LTspice simulation
explored the rectifier behaviour under different load conditions, including the capacitor
charge and discharge characteristics that are directly relevant to safety.

The key circuit-level concern is the capacitor energy store. A 4 kV capacitor bank holds
enough energy to cause lethal injury well after the mains supply is isolated. The simulation
work informed the discharge verification procedure: before any access is permitted, the
capacitors must be discharged through a bleed resistor and confirmed with a calibrated high-
voltage probe.

## Enclosure design in Fusion 360

The enclosure translates the circuit into a manufacturable, maintainable unit. The design in
Fusion 360 covered a sheet-metal layout in stainless steel, including the flat/net pattern
for fabrication, ventilation cutouts for the transformer and capacitor heat load, a power
entry cutout, and removable access panels for internal inspection and maintenance.

Component placement considered the internal layout: transformer at the base for stability,
capacitor bank isolated from the access panel side, and cabling routed clear of hot surfaces
and sharp edges. A parts list accompanied the drawing to support procurement.

## Safety framework

High-voltage enclosure design is not only a mechanical problem. The full safety framework
for the supply covers:

- **Mains isolation**: a lockable isolator on the incoming supply, so the cabinet cannot be
  energised while the access panel is open.
- **Capacitor discharge**: a bleed resistor across the capacitor bank and a mandatory
  discharge-and-verify step before any internal access.
- **Procedural controls**: short-pulse operation protocols to limit the energy in the
  transmission line during experiments; safe re-entry procedures that require two-person
  confirmation before restoring power after an interlock trip.
- **Physical barriers**: the enclosure isolates all high-voltage conductors from the operator
  surface; no bare conductors within reach of a standard access panel opening.

The enclosure was designed so that following the procedure is the path of least resistance:
every step that an operator must take to open the cabinet naturally passes through a safe
state first.

## What this demonstrates

The project combines three normally-separate disciplines — circuit simulation, mechanical
packaging, and safety systems engineering — into a single deliverable. For a university
project the combination is unusual; most safety design work at this level happens in industry.
The strongest signal is not the individual outputs but the approach: circuit behaviour,
physical form, and operational safety were designed together rather than sequentially.
