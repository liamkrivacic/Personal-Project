This project covers two practical pieces of microwave measurement hardware for AtomCraft's
RF transmission line: a cross-guide directional coupler and a waveguide-to-coax transition.
Both were designed and simulated in ANSYS HFSS around the system's 2.45 GHz operating
frequency in WR340 waveguide. The goal throughout was to turn a system measurement need into
a manufacturable component, not just an idealised simulation.

<Callout title="Headline results (simulated)">
In HFSS, the cross-guide coupler reaches roughly **40 dB coupling** with **28 dB directivity**
over a 0.12 GHz band centred near 2.45 GHz, and a **Monte Carlo tolerance study** confirms the
performance holds across realistic machining variation. These are simulation results, not
measured hardware figures.
</Callout>

## Directional coupler

The directional coupler exists to sample forward and reflected microwave power without
disrupting the high-power waveguide path. I designed a Moreno-style cross-guide coupler in
WR340, targeting clean power sampling across the operating band. In simulation the design
reaches roughly 40 dB coupling with 28 dB directivity over a 0.12 GHz bandwidth centred near
2.45 GHz.

Because a coupler is only useful if it survives manufacturing, I ran a Monte Carlo tolerance
study to check how the design behaves under realistic fabrication variation. This was the most
informative part of the work: it showed how coupling and directivity drift away from the
nominal result, which matters far more for a part that will actually be machined than a single
ideal simulation does.

## Waveguide-to-coax transition

The transition connects the waveguide hardware to coaxial instruments such as a VNA or
spectrum analyser. I designed a broad-wall probe transition with a tuned back short, again
simulated around 2.45 GHz. The simulated design shows very low insertion loss and strong
return loss, and the tolerance analysis indicated the match stays robust across the expected
range of manufacturing variation.

Together the two components form the measurement front end for the transmission line:
the coupler taps power for monitoring, and the transition routes that sample to standard
coaxial test equipment. The work demonstrates an end-to-end RF design loop — define the
measurement need, model the geometry, optimise in HFSS, and stress the design against
tolerances before committing to hardware.
