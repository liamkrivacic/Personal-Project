Most engineering portfolios do not include art. This one does, because the skills involved —
visual composition, sustained iteration, attention to detail, and communicating ideas to an
audience — transfer directly to engineering work, even if the domain does not.

## Recognition

The work was recognised through an **ARTEXPRESS nomination**, selection for exhibition at
**Tamworth Regional Gallery**, and an **NBN News interview**. ARTEXPRESS is a competitive
showcase of outstanding HSC major works; selection for gallery exhibition means the work was
assessed as exhibition-quality, not just school-level achievement.

## Why it belongs here

The strongest transfer from visual art to engineering is compositional judgement: knowing
what matters in a communication, what to include, what to leave out, and how to arrange
elements so the important information is seen first. This applies equally to a data
visualisation, a system diagram, a hardware layout, and a report structure.

The second transfer is process discipline. A major artwork takes months of sustained effort
across ideation, reference gathering, technical execution, and refinement. The ability to
maintain quality and direction across that arc — without external checkpoints forcing
progress — is the same skill that makes a long engineering project actually finish.
